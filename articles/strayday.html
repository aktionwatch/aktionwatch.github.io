Animal Welfare Workshop

Students at a primary school in Athens were given an opportunity to delve into animal welfare topics during a workshop facilitated by a member of Aktion Watch. The session, scheduled in line with World Stray Animal Day, focused on providing a foundational understanding of the care and protection of stray animals.

Eirini Chatzara 04 Apr · 3 min read
Share this

The event, held in a 6th grade classroom, used interactive discussion as its primary tool for engagement. An educator from Aktion Watch guided the discussion with the aid of a presentation that explored various aspects of animal welfare.

In a creative atmosphere, the students were introduced to the ideas of volunteering and the celebration of biodiversity. The workshop aimed to connect the students' understanding of their local environment to the broader topic of animal well-being, stressing the importance of a balanced ecosystem.

Aktion Watch's involvement provided the students with an educational experience that highlighted both the need for immediate action and contemplation of the larger ecological context. Practical ways for students to get involved in animal welfare were presented, matching their newfound knowledge with actionable steps.

Concluding the session, the students were left with clearer insight into the role they can play in supporting animal welfare. The workshop aimed to sow seeds of responsibility and care in the minds of the students.
